Creating a visual for counselors was never easy. It is an expression of the role of understanding humans and helping souls, so I wanted to find a form with the flexibility to change into anything. It was inspired by numerous sources such as sculpture and nature, and after several drawings, it was eventually depicted as a living line. "It's two-dimensional and three-dimensional at the same time," said story artist Afterton Corbin. "I wanted my face to look different depending on the angle. Character director Michael Comet also said, "We've created a new type of curve, and we've come up with a new technology that can transform the points. "We have also created functions that control the clear appearance of making very smooth shapes or adding fingers."
